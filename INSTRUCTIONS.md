# Human-AI Decision Record — User Instructions

---
### 2. What This Tool Does
This tool acts as an official, auditable ledger for documenting individual instances where AI was used to influence or make an institutional decision. It ensures that the specific context, the AI's output, and the final human judgment are recorded together for accountability.

---
### 3. Role in the Ecosystem
- **Phase:** Phase 2 — Governance Pipeline
- **Role:** Recording and auditing individual AI-influenced decisions.
- **Reference:** [../SYSTEM_OVERVIEW.md](../SYSTEM_OVERVIEW.md)

---
### 4. When to Use This Tool
- When a staff member uses AI to assist in grading, marking, or providing feedback on student work.
- When AI outputs are consulted during admissions processing or appeals.
- Whenever institutional policy demands a permanent record of AI involvement in a specific outcome.

---
### 5. Inputs
- Manual entry: Staff must input the context of the decision, the AI prompt used, the raw AI output, and their own overriding human rationale.

---
### 6. How to Use (Step-by-Step)
1. Initiate a new decision record entry.
2. Document the context (e.g., "Student Assessment Grading").
3. Paste or describe the prompt provided to the AI system.
4. Paste the raw, unmodified recommendation generated by the AI.
5. Most importantly, provide the "Human Rationale" — document exactly how the human reviewer interpreted, modified, or rejected the AI's suggestion.
6. Submit the record to generate an immutable log entry.

---
### 7. Key Outputs
- A transparent, exportable log demonstrating human accountability over an AI-assisted process.
- Institutional protection in the event of an appeal or audit.

---
### 8. How It Connects to Other Tools
- **Upstream:** Often triggered by processes previously approved via the **Risk Scanner**.
- **Downstream:** Aggregated logs from this tool can inform the **Maturity Assessment**.

---
### 9. Limitations
- Does not restrict the generation of AI outputs; it only records them after the fact.
- Relies on staff compliance and honesty to manually document the interaction.

---
### 10. Tips
- The "Human Rationale" field is the most legally and ethically significant part of this record. Always be specific about *why* you agreed or disagreed with the AI.

---
### 11. Capability and Governance
This tool supports both AI capability development and lightweight governance.
- **Capability Development**: The tool encourages users to reflect on how AI involvement affects the decision-making process, building professional judgement.
- **Governance Support**: Through optional fields, assumptions, risks, and rationale are made visible for audit and accountability.
- **Optional Fields**: All capability and governance notes are optional, supporting professional autonomy.
